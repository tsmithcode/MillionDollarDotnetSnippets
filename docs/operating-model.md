# MillionDollarDotnetSnippets Operating Model

This document implements the repo's autonomous dream-team model.
It is meant to be used for hiring, onboarding, and day-to-day decision making.

## Mission

Build MillionDollarDotnetSnippets into a commercially credible .NET asset whose title, content, and proof all match.

The project may evolve beyond its current structure if that makes it:

- more useful
- more ambitious
- faster to adopt
- more honest in market positioning

## Decision Model

The repo uses domain ownership, not committee ownership.

- The Senior .NET Architect owns codebase structure, package boundaries, API shape, examples architecture, dependency choices, test standards, and technical cuts.
- The Technical Writer / Developer Experience Editor owns README, documentation voice, naming clarity, learning flow, examples narrative, and removal of hype that weakens trust.
- The QA / Test Automation Engineer owns acceptance criteria, CI quality bars, support matrix, failure thresholds, and what cannot ship without proof.
- The Product / Positioning Lead owns title calibration, audience fit, offer framing, proof requirements, and repo-level commercial credibility.
- The Developer Relations / Community Lead owns community workflows, release education, contributor experience, and adoption loops.
- The Growth Product Marketer / Content Lead owns demand shaping, launch packaging, and persona-specific distribution messaging.
- The Design / Brand Systems Lead owns repo visual identity, README hierarchy, and brand consistency across shipped assets.
- The Accessibility & UX Quality Lead owns readability, navigation, accessibility standards, and premium-pass usability review.
- The Platform / Release Engineer owns entrypoint clarity, CI maturity, packaging reliability, and release ergonomics.

## Authority Rules

- Domain owners can add, remove, rename, reorganize, or replace assets inside their domain without routine founder approval.
- The team is allowed to delete weak work.
- Protecting the current repo shape is not a goal.
- Any major change must improve at least one of these outcomes: stronger proof, higher usefulness, faster adoption, or more honest positioning.
- The founder intervenes only if a proposal abandons the mission instead of improving it.
- The README and repo vision are fully revisable by the team.
- The title is the anchor; the current wording is not.

## Tie-Breaking

- Cross-cutting technical conflicts are resolved by the Senior .NET Architect.
- Positioning, audience, and repo promise conflicts are resolved by the Product / Positioning Lead.
- When a decision affects both technical truth and market truth, the Architect and Product Lead produce a single written decision with tradeoffs and expected upside.
- README, narrative, and visual-direction conflicts are resolved by Product, Writer, and Design together, with Product holding final positioning authority.

## Weekly Operating Cadence

- Monday: each domain owner posts current priorities, cuts, additions, and known blockers.
- Midweek: cross-functional review for conflicts between code, docs, quality, and positioning.
- Friday: publish shipped changes, rejected ideas, and proof added that week.

## Non-Negotiables

- Public claims must be backed by shipped evidence in the repo.
- Every shipped area must have a named owner.
- Docs must reflect reality, not aspiration.
- Quality gates may block release if proof is missing.
- Ambition is encouraged; drift is not.

## First-Week Deliverables for Every Hire

- a 30-day vision memo using the repo template
- a keep / remove / add / radically improve list for their domain
- one major decision they would make immediately
- one proof gap they believe currently weakens credibility

## Success Definition

Success means the repo becomes a project that senior specialists want to own:

- real authority
- visible authorship
- low micromanagement
- fast decisions
- clear accountability
