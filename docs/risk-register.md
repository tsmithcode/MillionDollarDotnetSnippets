# Risk Register

This document tracks the most important product and company-operating risks for Million Dollar Dot Net Snippets.

## Active risks

| Risk | Severity | Current status | Owner | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Framework depth lags site quality | High | Active | Senior .NET Architect / Library Owner | Keep flagship proof slices shipping faster than visual or narrative expansion. |
| Runtime proof gap in current environment | Medium | Active | Enterprise Workflow Delivery Lead | Re-run the flagship .NET slice in a clean runtime environment and record the output. |
| Monetization is clearer than market validation | Medium | Active | Product / Positioning Lead | Validate open-core and premium-module demand through traction or consulting pull. |
| Trust posture still uses compatibility CSP | Medium | Accepted | Security and Trust Lead | Continue tightening CSP only when it will not break stable production execution. |
| Executive reporting decays into stale docs | Medium | Active | Executive Product Operations Lead | Treat scorecard, risk register, and investor package as recurring artifacts, not one-time deliverables. |

## Closed or reduced risks

| Risk | Status | Why it is lower now |
|---|---|---|
| Browser confidence was only implied | Reduced | The browser gate now covers core flows across Chromium, WebKit, and mobile. |
| Accessibility was premium-only, not proven | Reduced | Skip-link focus, reduced-motion parity, and keyboard behavior are release-tested. |
| Product category was too vague | Reduced | The leadership and proof surfaces now explain the framework as a consultant acceleration product. |
